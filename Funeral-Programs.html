Funeral Programs

Funeral programs  (sometimes called   funeral brochures,  funeral pamphlets or  funeral bulletins) are the printed document that is given out funeral or memorial services that outlines the key points in the funeral or memorial service and summarizes the life achievements of your deceased loved one. Memorial and  funeral programs can also be distributed electronically, such as through email or posted to social media and memorial websites.

The family usually gathers and prepares the information for the  funeral program.  They may work with the funeral home, church or graphic designer to create it, or they may use  funeral program template from The  Funeral Program Site to create unique, beautiful and personalized custom  funeral programs on their own computer and home printer. Funeral Programs

Funeral Programs usually contain the following information Funeral Program Cover photo and background graphics or other clipart with a descriptive caption,  Funeral Program Order of Service or Service Outline, A Descriptive Obituary or Life Sketch, Funeral Poems and Scriptutres, Memorial Photo collages and Memorial clipart. In addition to written information, other design elements can be added to your funeral program to give the program a more polished and personalized look and feel of your funeral program.  Consider adding photos of family and friends and photo collages to your program to turn your program into a special momento or keepsake.  You can also add memorial clipart such as fancy borders, and frames around photos and text, clipart that helps describe your loved one's personality such as a religious image or a favorite flower.  Flourishes and separators can be added to visual clarity and white space to your programs, and give an organized and neat appearance.

The back of the funeral program may include a memorial poem, favorite song or prayer.  The final section of the funeral program may also include a personal quote from a family member, listing of pall bearers (men that carry the casket), flower bearers (women traditionally carry flowers and place them on the alter) or special acknowledgements (short thank you from the family).  When designing a funeral program, it is important to remember that the funeral program is a keepsake that represents the life of your loved one. The funeral programs should be detailed and share the unique personality traits of your loved one.

Online Funeral Programs

Online funeral programs are digital tools that allow individuals to create and customize funeral programs or memorial service programs online. These programs typically include information about the deceased, such as their name, date of birth, date of death, and a brief biography or obituary. They also include details about the funeral or memorial service, such as the date, time, and location, as well as any readings, prayers, or songs that will be included in the service.

Online funeral programs offer several advantages over traditional printed programs. First, they are more convenient and accessible. Instead of having to physically design and print programs, individuals can create them online from the comfort of their own homes. This is especially beneficial for those who may be grieving and find it difficult to leave their homes or engage in physical tasks.

Additionally, online funeral programs allow for easy customization. Users can choose from a variety of templates and designs, and can easily add or remove sections, change fonts and colors, and upload photos or other personal touches. This allows for a more personalized and meaningful program that truly reflects the life and personality of the deceased.

Another advantage of online funeral programs is that they can be easily shared and distributed. Once the program is created, it can be shared with family members, friends, and other attendees via email or social media. This eliminates the need for printing and mailing physical copies, saving time and resources.

Furthermore, online funeral programs can be updated and edited even after they have been shared. If there are any last-minute changes to the service, such as a change in location or additional readings, the program can be easily updated and redistributed to all attendees. This ensures that everyone has the most up-to-date information and can follow along with the service seamlessly.
